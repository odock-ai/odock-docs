# Usage Analytics

The card at the top of the Usage Records page is for the *how is my organisation trending* question. It loads recent usage records, applies your filters, and renders four KPI cards plus three charts.


## Workflow

<Steps>

<Step>
Open your organisation workspace.
</Step>

<Step>
Open **Usage Records** from the sidebar.
</Step>

<Step>
Pick a **Date range** (7d, 30d, 90d, 12 months, or all loaded).
</Step>

![Usage Analytics card on the Usage Records page](/gifs/obs-usagechart-p1.gif)


<Step>
Use **Status**, **Provider**, and **Model** to narrow what the analytics consider.
</Step>

<Step>
Use **Team**, **User**, and **API Key** to attribute the analytics to a specific principal.
</Step>

<Step>
Pick a **Breakdown** dimension and a **Metric** to drive the bar chart.
</Step>

<Step>
Use the **Series** dropdown on the time-series chart to toggle requests, cost, and token series.
</Step>

![Usage Analytics card on the Usage Records page](/gifs/obs-recordchart-p2.gif)


</Steps>



## What The Cards And Charts Show

| Element | Reads |
| --- | --- |
| **Total requests** | Number of records after filters. |
| **Total cost** | Sum of cost in USD. |
| **Avg latency** | Mean latency in milliseconds. |
| **Success rate** | Share of records with a 2xx/3xx status. |
| Time-series chart | Daily trend for the selected series. |
| Status donut | Success, error, and unknown share for the selected metric. |
| Breakdown bar chart | Top categories for the chosen breakdown dimension. |

![Usage Analytics chart](/gifs/obs-chart.gif)

## Tips
<Callout type="tip">
The **Clear filters** button resets everything back to defaults.
</Callout>


<Callout type="tip">
Set **Breakdown** to **API Key** and **Metric** to **Cost (USD)** to find the keys driving spend in the selected window. That points you at the right key to tighten with a [budget](/docs/management/budgets) or [quota](/docs/management/quotas).
</Callout>

<Callout type="tip">
The Series choice is remembered between visits. Add the cost series alongside requests once, and it stays on for next time.
</Callout>
